07 — Common questions

Questions investors ask before the first message.

Can a foreigner buy property in Portugal?
Yes. Portugal puts no nationality restrictions on real estate ownership. Non-residents need a Portuguese tax number (NIF) and in most cases a Portuguese bank account. Both can be arranged remotely with a power of attorney. The legal process is the same as for residents.
Should I buy through a company or in my own name?
It depends on your tax residency, your holding horizon and your exit plan. An asset deal is a direct purchase of the property — IMT plus stamp duty on the price. A share deal — buying the company that holds the property — can change the tax position but inherits the company's history. The right structure is decided before any offer is signed, not after.
What does legal due diligence cover?
Title at the Land Registry. Tax status at the Finanças. Urban-planning licences. Existing leases and encumbrances. Mortgages, condominium and utility debts. For developments, the alvará and the planning framework. The point is to make sure what is being sold matches what is registered, what is taxed and what can legally be used.
What taxes apply on a Portuguese property purchase?
On purchase: IMT (transfer tax), stamp duty at 0.8% of the price, notary and registry fees. Annually: IMI (municipal property tax) and AIMI above certain thresholds. Exact rates depend on property type, use and value. The full cost should be modelled before you sign the promissory contract.
How long does a Portuguese transaction take?
For a clean residential deal with a prepared buyer, six to ten weeks from offer to deed. Complex transactions — development land, multi-fraction buildings, properties with title or planning issues — take three to six months. The two most common delays are bank financing on the buyer side and undisclosed registry or planning issues on the seller side.
How does the Golden Visa work in 2026?
Since the 2023 reform, direct real estate investment no longer qualifies. The current routes are qualifying investment funds, capital transfer, scientific research and job creation. The residency benefit is still attractive — a five-year path to citizenship with minimal stay requirements — but property and residency are now separate decisions.
How do your fees work?
